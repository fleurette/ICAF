About the Program

HOPE FOR PEACE
A failure to bring peace pales every other success. Children ask why their world is torn by conflict and violence, why countries and cultures cannot co-exist on this loneliest in the multiverse.

Strategists explicate the national security interests, politicians the geopolitical futures, business people the economic necessity, and historians the antecedents of the growing ethnic and religious intolerance.

Neurobiologists and neuroscientists are making notable advances in understanding how the anatomy and physiology of the brain and nervous system determine human proclivity for sadism and savagery. Scientists in the field of neuroepigenetics are studying how one's life experiences lead to changes in the brain and how those changes make humans diabolical, inhumane, or apathetic, and even silent or blind.

The hope for peace resides in children. Just one generation can upstage the human history of wars, occupations, and appropriations.
SEEDING EMPATHY
Over the past 25 years, ICAF has developed exceptional expertise in seeding mutual empathy in children. We do this at the World Children's Festival, where young artists from across the globe congregate for three days at The National Mall in Washington, D.C.
OVERARCHING GOAL
ICAF organizes Peace through Art Programs as interventions that tap into children's innate creative resources so they can imagine their enemy as human beings not so different from themselves and begin to visualize peaceful co-existence and collaboration. Our overarching goal is to curtail the transgenerational transmission of trauma and hatred to free the children from a harrowing past for a tranquil and mindful future.

Please help us help the children to co-create a peaceful world.

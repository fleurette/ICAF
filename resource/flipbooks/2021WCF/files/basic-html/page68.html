                                    Necessity is the mother of invention. That’s a pretty famous 
saying that means we get quite creative when our life needs 
us to. I like this saying. It reminds me that our human minds 
can rise to the challenges that life throws at us.
Now, as smart as we are, we weren’t the first animals to use the 
power of creativity to solve problems. In fact, do you know who 
invented flying? Bugs! Do you know who invented paper? Bugs! 
Do you know who invented computers? Well, that one was 
humans, but bugs have been studied to help make computers 
more efficient! There are millions of types of bugs that have 
been thrown millions of challenges, and maybe they’ve solved 
a few in ways we haven’t thought of . . . but how could I show 
people that?
The 2021 World Children’s Festival provided the perfect 
opportunity to test the creativity of humans against the world 
of bugs. I gave children (and some adults) several random 
objects ranging from hair combs to chopsticks to big metal 
cookie containers and then gave them a simple challenge. I 
asked them to invent a new sound using only the objects I had 
given them…any sound their creative minds could conjure. And 
then came the chaos. 
There were BANGS and CLANGS, PLUCKS and POPS, 
CRACKS and RATTLES! It was a whole symphony of unheard 
sounds! “But wait,” I thought, “I’ve heard these sounds before 
. . .” The banging of chopsticks on the table reminded me of 
the drumming of jumping spiders. Those pops of the cookie 
container lid sounded a lot like the pops of cicada tymbals 
screeching in the trees. And the rattles from the hair comb 
were just like the summer songs of crickets gently scraping 
their wings together. These sounds that we were making had 
already been invented long ago by the world’s bugs.
“Well, is there a noise we could make that bugs haven’t already 
invented?” This question was soon to be answered. One child 
quietly sitting in his chair was watching, waiting, working the 
creative gears in his own mind, trying to invent a truly new 
noise. When it was his turn to create a sound, I asked what 
noise he had invented. After a thoughtful moment, he slowly 
reached below his chair to the gravel sidewalk below, picked up 
a handful of rocks, and then lightly sprinkled them all over the 
cookie container lid . . . and with that, I was stumped! I could 
not, even after digging into the depths of my bug-fueled mind, 
come up with a bug that had invented that sound! This kiddo 
took a challenge inspired by bugs and solved it with creativity.
Bugs have been inventing solutions for millions of years and 
can help inspire our own creativity in all sorts of areas ranging 
from music to science, from art to technology, and any spaces 
in between. Bugs can provide  direct solutions  to solve our 
problems or can even just set us in a new direction. Bugs can 
carry the weight of an entire issue or simply be the little push 
we need to get started. Whatever kind of creativity you inspire 
in yourselves or others, if you ever need a little help, bugs 
have you covered.

                                    Come with me to the 6th World Children’s Festival (WCF). For 
the next several minutes, through the pages of this magazine, 
you will experience extraordinary art, learn from creative 
workshops, and meet exceptional youth. 
This year, the 6th WCF opened July 30 at noon, on the National 
Mall. The US Army Joint Color Guard unfurled the flag with 
pride and precision. Anupriya Roy of the Levine School of Music 
sang the US national anthem. Eleven-year-old Evie Felten of 
North Carolina followed, with Joseph Haydn’s “Serenade” on 
flute. The colors and music filled the air with a reminder of the 
freedom we hold dear as we creatively work toward global 
understanding and a peaceful world.
The WCF is produced quadrennially by the International Child 
Art Foundation (ICAF). It is a grand celebration that gathers 
children from across the globe for an experience in creativity, 
unity,  and diversity.  Preparation for each WCF  begins well 
in advance, with the Arts Olympiad  contest. In classrooms 
worldwide, the Arts Olympiad lesson plans introduce students 
to the “artist-athlete ideal,” combining a creative mind with a 
healthy body. Students then create art inspired by the theme 
“My Favorite Sport.” Winning art is selected in schools and 
cities across the globe. At the WCF the winning art from the 
US and more than twenty other countries is on display. It offers 
a window into the hearts and minds of youth around the world 
and speaks to their creative potential.
There is excitement in the air as children arrive on the Mall, 
ready to participate in hands-on workshops and applaud the 
carefully selected performing artists as they showcase their 
talents on the World Stage. 
The three-day WCF begins with Health and Environment Day, 
followed by Creativity and Imagination Day, and ends with Peace 
and Leadership Day. The workshops and activities promote 
the holistic nature of STEAMS education, integrating science, 
technology, engineering, arts, math, and sports to prepare youth 
for success as innovators and leaders. The festival inspires 
wonder and inquiry as children learn to work through the creative 
process and embrace collaboration.
At  this  year’s  festival,  five-time  Olympian  and  sports  artist, 
Liston Bochette, came from Fort Myers, Florida, to address 
the children. He brought greetings from H.S.H. Prince Albert II 
of Monaco, who was attending the Olympic Games in Tokyo.
